## Content Types for GEO

Not all content formats are equally effective for AI search citation. AI engines strongly prefer factual, structured, and authoritative content.

### Recommended Content Types

These formats have the highest AI citation rates:

| Content Type | Why It Works | AI Citation Potential |
|---|---|---|
| **In-depth guides & tutorials** | Comprehensive coverage that AI can extract multi-step answers from | Very High |
| **Original research reports** | First-party data that AI treats as primary sources | Very High |
| **Expert opinions & analysis** | Demonstrates E-E-A-T signals AI models prioritize | High |
| **Data-driven articles** | Statistics and benchmarks AI engines frequently cite | High |
| **FAQ & Q&A content** | Directly matches conversational AI query patterns | High |
| **Term definitions & glossaries** | Self-contained explanations ideal for AI extraction | High |
| **Comparison articles** | Structured side-by-side analysis AI uses for recommendation queries | Medium-High |
| **Case studies** | Real-world evidence with specific results and metrics | Medium-High |
| **How-to step-by-step guides** | Numbered procedures AI can present as complete answers | Medium-High |

### Content Types Most Likely to Earn AI Citations

These five categories are what LLMs actively seek to cite:

| Content Type | Why LLMs Cite It | Agent Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Original Research** | LLMs use your data to support specific claims | Include unique data, methodology, and findings |
| **Case Studies** | LLMs cite real-world results to back recommendations | Structure with problem → approach → measurable outcome |
| **Thought Leadership** | LLMs cite unique perspectives to provide diverse viewpoints | Present an original thesis with supporting evidence |
| **News Content** | LLMs can't rely on training data for recent events | Include dates, named sources, and specific facts |
| **Brand Content** | LLMs trust you as the most accurate source about your own brand | Ensure product/service pages are factual and complete |

### Writing for LLM Extraction ("Chunkable" Content)

AI models extract and recombine content fragments. Every paragraph and section must work in isolation:

```
RULES for LLM-friendly writing:

1. Each paragraph should make sense if extracted alone
   BAD:  "As mentioned above, this approach works well."
   GOOD: "The structured data approach improves AI citation rates by 85%."

2. Use descriptive subheadings with proper HTML heading tags
   BAD:  <h2>More Details</h2>
   GOOD: <h2>How Schema Markup Improves AI Citation Rates</h2>

3. Mention relevant entities and concepts explicitly
   BAD:  "The platform supports this."
   GOOD: "ChatGPT Search supports real-time web citation from indexed pages."

4. Avoid poetic, vague, or ambiguous language
   BAD:  "In the ever-evolving landscape of digital transformation..."
   GOOD: "AI search traffic grew 357% year-over-year as of June 2025."

5. Add useful images with descriptive alt text for multimedia citations
```

### Content Types to Avoid for GEO

These formats perform poorly in AI search:

```
AVOID for GEO:
- Thin content (< 300 words with no depth)
- Duplicate or syndicated content without original value
- Purely sales-oriented or promotional content
- Outdated information that hasn't been updated
- Unsourced opinions without evidence or credentials
- Clickbait headlines with shallow content
- Content behind paywalls (AI crawlers can't access it)
- Image-heavy content with little extractable text
```

### Formatting for Maximum Extraction

Structure content so AI engines can extract useful snippets:

```markdown
## What is [Topic]?

[One-sentence definition that stands alone as a complete answer.]

[2-3 sentences of elaboration with a key statistic or fact.]

### Key Benefits

1. **[Benefit 1]** — [One-sentence explanation]
2. **[Benefit 2]** — [One-sentence explanation]
3. **[Benefit 3]** — [One-sentence explanation]

### How It Works

1. [Step 1]: [Clear instruction]
2. [Step 2]: [Clear instruction]
3. [Step 3]: [Clear instruction]

### [Topic] vs [Alternative]

| Feature | [Topic] | [Alternative] |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| [Feature 1] | [Value] | [Value] |
| [Feature 2] | [Value] | [Value] |
```

### Content Length Guidelines

AI engines don't have a strict preference for length, but content depth matters:

- **Definitions/Glossary entries**: 100-300 words (concise, authoritative)
- **FAQ answers**: 150-400 words per answer (complete but focused)
- **Blog posts/Articles**: 1,500-3,000 words (comprehensive coverage)
- **In-depth guides**: 3,000-7,000 words (exhaustive topic coverage)
- **Research reports**: 2,000-10,000 words (methodology + findings + analysis)

The key metric is NOT word count but **information density** — every paragraph should add unique value.
