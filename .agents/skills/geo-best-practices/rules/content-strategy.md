## Core Content Principles for GEO

All content optimized for generative engines MUST follow these four pillars:

### 1. Authority

AI search engines prioritize content from authoritative sources. Every piece of content should:

- Cite reliable sources and research data with links
- Include original research, first-party data, or unique datasets
- Demonstrate professional credentials and real-world experience
- Reference specific studies, statistics, and named experts

```markdown
<!-- GOOD: Authoritative content with citations -->
According to a 2025 Backlinko analysis, website traffic from LLMs increased
800% year-over-year (Q2 2024 → Q2 2025). Our internal data from 500+ client
sites confirms this trend, with an average 340% increase in AI-referred visits.

<!-- BAD: Vague, unsourced claims -->
AI search traffic is growing a lot and many websites are seeing more visits
from AI platforms.
```

### 2. Quotability

AI engines extract and cite concise, well-structured passages. Optimize for quotability:

- Write clear, self-contained definitions and explanations in 1-3 sentences
- Use "quotable blocks" — paragraphs that stand alone as complete answers
- Lead paragraphs with the key fact or definition, then elaborate
- Provide specific statistics, numbers, and named facts

```markdown
<!-- GOOD: Easily extractable by AI -->
**Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** is an optimization strategy that
improves content visibility in AI-powered search engines such as ChatGPT,
Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Unlike traditional SEO, GEO focuses
on authority, quotability, and factual accuracy rather than keyword density
and backlink volume.

<!-- BAD: Buried definition, hard to extract -->
In the world of digital marketing, there are many strategies that businesses
use. One of these newer approaches, which some people call GEO, is about
making your content show up when people use AI tools to search for things.
```

### 3. Comprehensiveness

AI models favor content that thoroughly covers a topic. Comprehensive content should:

- Cover all major aspects and subtopics
- Anticipate and answer follow-up questions within the content
- Provide step-by-step how-to guidance where applicable
- Include both introductory explanations and advanced details
- Address common misconceptions

### 4. Structure

Well-structured content is easier for AI systems to parse and cite:

- Use clear heading hierarchies (H1 → H2 → H3) with descriptive headings
- Organize information with bullet lists and numbered steps
- Use comparison tables for multi-option topics
- Implement Schema.org markup (see [structured-data.md](./structured-data.md))
- Place key definitions and answers near the top of sections

```html
<!-- GOOD: Clear heading hierarchy -->
<h1>Complete Guide to Generative Engine Optimization</h1>
  <h2>What is GEO?</h2>
  <h2>GEO vs SEO vs AEO</h2>
    <h3>Key Differences</h3>
    <h3>When to Use Each Approach</h3>
  <h2>How to Implement GEO</h2>
    <h3>Content Strategy</h3>
    <h3>Technical Requirements</h3>
```

## Content Writing Checklist

Before publishing any GEO-optimized content, verify:

- [ ] Unique value is provided (not just rehashing existing content)
- [ ] Verifiable facts and data are included with sources
- [ ] Clear heading hierarchy is used throughout
- [ ] Author information is complete and credible
- [ ] Authoritative external sources are cited
- [ ] Core user questions are directly answered
- [ ] Easily quotable paragraphs exist for key definitions
- [ ] Content reads well both as a whole and in extracted snippets
